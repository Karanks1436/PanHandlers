ABOUT PANHANDLERS

A panhandler is a person who asks others for money or food, usually without offering anything in return. Panhandling is often seen as a form of begging or soliciting and is commonly associated with homelessness, poverty, or economic hardship. Panhandlers may stand on street corners, near busy intersections, or in public places where they are likely to encounter pedestrians or passersby. The motivations and circumstances of panhandlers can vary widely, ranging from individuals experiencing homelessness to those seeking temporary financial assistance.

OUR SERVICES
